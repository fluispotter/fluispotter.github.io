Stress-Free, Automated PK-PD Profiles

The Fluispotter wearable blood sampling system enables precise blood sampling for pharmacokinetic (PK) and pharmacodynamic (PD) studies in large animals, offering stress-free blood collection and enhanced animal welfare.

The Fluispotter, revolutionizes pharmacokinetic (PK) and pharmacodynamic (PD) studies in preclinical settings with large animals. Designed to streamline sampling processes, this wearable automated blood sampling system ensures precise, high-quality data collection while prioritizing animal welfare.

By combining advanced automation, remote capabilities, and animal-centric design, Fluispotter supports the well-being of research subjects while delivering reliable data for drug development.

Key Features

  • Automated Catheter Maintenance: Features built-in catheter flushing and maintenance to ensure consistent performance and prevent blockages.
  • Flexible Study Execution: Load predefined study protocols for automated execution or trigger on-demand sampling through the remote interface.
  • Versatile Access Options: Supports multiple catheter configurations, including catheter-in-catheter, subcutaneous ports, transcutaneous ports, or direct vein access, tailored to study needs.
  • High Sample Capacity: Collects up to 50 samples per study, with each sample at 10 µL, enabling detailed time-course analysis.
  • Extended Runtime: Operates continuously for up to 48 hours, supporting long-duration PK/PD studies without interruption
  • Remote Monitoring and Control: Offers real-time feedback and full remote control, allowing researchers to monitor study progress and adjust sampling protocols via a user-friendly interface.
  • No Fluid Handling: Integrated anticoagulant system eliminates manual fluid handling, simplifying workflows and reducing contamination risks.
  • No-Carry-Over Design: Ensures clean sampling with zero carry-over between samples, maintaining data integrity.
  • Wearable Design: Lightweight and optimized for large animals (e.g., swine, canines, non-human primates), allowing natural movement and minimizing stress.
  • Dried Blood Spot Sampling: Collects up to 50 dried blood spots over 36 hours, ideal for downstream bioanalysis and storage.
  • Super Simple User Interface: Streamlined operation with an "Unpack, Connect, Activate, and Sample" workflow, minimizing training and setup time.

Applications

  • Cardiovascular and telemetry studies for drug saftey.
  • Drug absorption, distribution, metabolism, and excretion (ADME) studies.
  • Dose-response and efficacy assessments in PD models.
  • Biomarker analysis and therapeutic monitoring in large animal models.

Why Researchers Choose Fluispotter?

For pharmaceutical researchers conducting PK/PD studies in preclinical large animal models, the system from offers a stress-free, efficient, and ethical solution. By combining advanced automation, remote capabilities, and animal-centric design, it ensures high-quality data while supporting the well-being of research subjects, ultimately accelerating drug development timelines. Researchers choose Fluispotter because of:

  • Efficiency: Frees up researcher time by automating repetitive sampling tasks, allowing focus on data analysis and study design.
  • Scalability: Suitable for large animal models, supporting diverse preclinical programs.
  • Regulatory Compliance: Aligns with 3Rs principles (Replacement, Reduction, Refinement) by minimizing animal stress and optimizing study outcomes.
  • Versatility: Adaptable to various study designs, including single-dose, multi-dose, and extended-release PK/PD evaluations.

Technical Specifications

  • Runtime: Up to 48 hours of continuous operation.
  • Sample Volume: 10 µL per sample, with a capacity of 50 samples.
  • Sampling Duration: Supports extended sampling up to 48 hours for dried blood spot collection.
  • Remote Capabilities: Fully remotely controlled and monitored with real-time data feedback.
  • Catheter Maintenance: Automated flushing and upkeep for reliable performance.
  • Sample Integrity: No-carry-over design with built-in anticoagulant for clean, uncontaminated samples.

Publications

The following peer-reviewed publications and abstracts highlight the performance, safety, and applications of the Fluispotter device in various research settings:

  1. Adhikari, K. B., et al. "Fluispotter, a novel automated and wearable device for accurate volume serial dried blood spot sampling." Bioanalysis, vol. 12, no. 10, 2020, pp. 665–681.

    Evaluates the Fluispotter’s sampling performance, demonstrating high accuracy and precision in serial dried blood spot sampling for cortisol analysis across a range of hematocrit values.

  2. Krogh, J., et al. "Validation of a novel automated system, Fluispotter®, for serial sampling of dried blood spots." Endocrine Connections, vol. 12, no. 7, 2023, e230087.

    Assesses the safety and performance of Fluispotter in healthy human subjects, confirming reliable cortisol profiles with minimal discomfort, despite some challenges with blood flow clogging.

  3. Krogh, J., et al. "Serial sampling of dried blood spots collected by a novel automated body-worn system, Fluispotter®." Endocrine Abstracts, vol. 90, 2023, P90.

    Presented at the 25th European Congress of Endocrinology (ECE2023), this abstract details Fluispotter’s application in automated serial sampling, highlighting safety and acceptability in human subjects.

  4. Jensen, A. L., et al. "A novel device for serial venous blood sampling in a canine model." Journal of Pharmacological and Toxicological Methods, vol. 99, 2019, 106595.

    Demonstrates Fluispotter’s performance in a canine model, showing no stress-related changes in blood chemistry or behavior, suitable for preclinical pharmacology and toxicology studies.

  5. Velschow, S., et al. "Technical and animal welfare perspectives of portable automated blood sampling with Fluispotter® in Göttingen Minipigs." Scandinavian Journal of Laboratory Animal Science, vol. 42, 2016.

    Explores the technical performance and welfare benefits of Fluispotter in Göttingen minipigs, emphasizing its stress-free sampling capabilities. (Note: Full text may not be available; contact the journal for access.)
